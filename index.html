Artist Statement

The work begins in silence — with the quality of light, the weight of air, a surface that has known use.

Jadon Schwahn is a painter and draftsman working primarily in oil and graphite. Raised in Montana, his practice is shaped by the particular quality of light in the northern plains — long shadows, overcast mornings, the subtle drama of unremarkable places.

His paintings tend toward quiet: domestic interiors, open landscapes, figures caught in stillness. He is drawn to the way ordinary things accumulate meaning through close observation — a table, a window, a stand of cottonwoods at the edge of a field.

He works from direct observation whenever possible, and the drawings are always the beginning.
